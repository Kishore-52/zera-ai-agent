# Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedure: Hydraulic Press HP-01

## 1. Preparation
Identify all hazardous energy sources associated with Hydraulic Press HP-01. Notify all affected personnel that the machine will be shut down for maintenance.

## 2. Machine Shutdown
Shut down the machine using normal stopping procedures (e.g., depress the stop button).

## 3. Energy Isolation
- **Electrical**: Turn off the main electrical breaker.
- **Hydraulic**: Close the main hydraulic isolation valve.
- **Pneumatic**: Close the pneumatic supply valve to the workpiece clamp.

## 4. Lock and Tag
Apply designated locks and tags to all energy isolation points identified in step 3.

## 5. Stored-Energy Release
- **Hydraulic**: Relieve stored pressure in the accumulator according to the approved hydraulic pressure-release procedure.
- **Pneumatic**: Bleed off any remaining pneumatic pressure.

## 6. Mechanical Restraint
If the ram is raised, install the rated mechanical ram safety block to prevent gravitational descent.

## 7. Zero-Energy Verification
Verify that all energy sources have been isolated and residual energy is within safe limits (Electrical <= 5V, Hydraulic <= 1 bar, Pneumatic <= 1 bar). Watch for pressure rebound.

## 8. Try-Start Verification
Ensure no personnel are exposed, then attempt to start the machine using the normal controls. Ensure no movement occurs during try-start. Return controls to the "off" position.

## 9. Authorized Human Review
Submit all evidence and sensor readings to an authorized supervisor for review. Wait for approval before commencing maintenance.

## 10. Restoration and Restart
After maintenance is complete, check the area, remove all tools, remove locks/tags, and restore energy sources systematically.
